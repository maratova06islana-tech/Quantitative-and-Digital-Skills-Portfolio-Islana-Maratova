# Customer Base Audit

## Project Overview
This project analyzes the customer base of a business to understand how different customer segments contribute to total revenue and profit. Customers are divided into 10 deciles based on revenue contribution. The analysis identifies high-value customers and their behavior, providing insights for retention and marketing strategies.

## Data & Methods
- Dataset: 31,797 customers with 61,260 transactions, total revenue $325,600,660, and total profit $156,016,135.  
- Methods:
  - Decile segmentation based on revenue
  - Calculations: average revenue per customer, average profit per customer, order frequency (AOF), order value, and profit margin
  - Cumulative revenue and profit percentages
  - Charts for revenue, profit, and customer behavior trends

## Key Insights
- Top 10% of customers contribute ~40% of revenue and profit.  
- Average revenue per customer declines sharply from top to bottom decile.  
- High-value customers purchase more frequently (AOF = 5.5) than lower-value customers (AOF = 1.0).  
- Profit margins remain consistent (42–49%), so differences in profit are primarily due to volume.
